Message Frequency & Awareness Strategy Lab

Build and critique a media plan by distinguishing messages sent from message frequency actually received. The exercise uses the logic from the slides on message frequency, awareness, and the idea that nearly all campaigns send more messages than the target audience receives.

Scenario

PulseBoost Launch Campaign

Role: You are advising a brand team launching PulseBoost, a functional beverage aimed at graduate students and young professionals. The team is proud that it can send a large number of impressions, but the VP of Marketing worries that the audience will not actually experience enough effective frequency to build awareness.
Your task is to analyze the exposure distribution, estimate actual average frequency, and choose the best managerial response.

MBA Interpretation

How to reason about the output

  • High messages sent do not prove high frequency received.
  • Awareness often depends on a meaningful share of the audience crossing an effective-frequency threshold.
  • A manager may need to trade off reach against frequency, not simply maximize gross impressions.
  • The best next action depends on whether the problem is distribution, creative quality, or insufficient weight.
